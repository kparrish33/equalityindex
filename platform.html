How it works

Three panels.
One decision.

Every submission moves through the same structured workflow — context in, score computed, decision recorded. Nothing is unlogged. Nothing is unversioned.

Panel 01
Context
Property and intake data submitted by the broker — read-only from the underwriter's view. Parcel ID, address, ownership, and environmental context are locked at submission and cannot be modified downstream.
Property address + parcel ID
Owner + broker identity
Environmental context flags
Inspection date (if available)
Panel 02
eQuality Score
The score is computed from the submitted data using the scoring formula. Domain decomposition shows the contribution of each risk area. The output is deterministic — the same inputs always produce the same score under the same model version.
e[score] on 300–850 scale
Risk tier + capital action
Six domain scores (RR, CL, RM, SC, OR, DU)
Model version + hash
Panel 03
Decision
The underwriter records reasoning and selects an action. Both outcomes are logged and the submission state updates accordingly. Every decision is retained and traceable.
Underwriter notes (up to 500 chars)

Submission States

Every submission has
a clear state

No submission sits in an undefined state. Every stage is named, logged, and traceable to the action that caused it.

Intake
Submission received. Property and broker data locked. Awaiting score computation.
Due Diligence
Score computed. Under active underwriter review. Decision pending.
Proposal Sent
Underwriter approved. Score frozen. Proposal routed to broker.
Declined by UW
Underwriter declined. Decision recorded with notes.
Closed
Submission resolved. Full audit trail preserved and retained for review.
Score Output

What every score
includes

Every issued eQuality score carries a complete set of structured outputs — not just a number.

analytics
eScore + Risk Tier
The external score on a 300–850 scale, formatted as e[score], with a risk tier classification (Tier 1–5) and a capital action: proceed, reprice, escalate diligence, or decline.
bar_chart
Domain Decomposition
Individual scores across all six risk domains — Regulatory, Contamination, Remediation, Site Context, Operator, and Data Uncertainty — showing the contribution of each area to the final score.
verified
Confidence Level
A data quality signal — High, Moderate, or Low — reflecting the completeness and reliability of the underlying evidence. Separate from the score value.
fact_check
Evidence Posture
A breakdown of activated scoring inputs by evidence tier — showing whether the score is driven by authoritative Tier 1 records or lower-tier inferred signals.
warning
Tail Overlay Flags
Structured metadata flags for latent risk exposures — including emerging contaminants and plume migration — for downstream actuarial analysis and reserving decisions.
history
Immutable Audit Record
Every score is issued with a timestamp and hash. Decisions are logged and retained. Historical scores are never retroactively modified.
